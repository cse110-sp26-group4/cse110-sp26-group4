# CSE 110 Team Contract

## 1. Team Purpose and Objectives
Our team’s purpose is to collaboratively design, develop, and deliver high-quality, well-documented software while strengthening both our technical and teamwork skills.

Our objectives are to:
- Build a functional and maintainable software product
- Apply industry-standard development practices (e.g., version control, testing, documentation)
- Improve collaboration, communication, and problem-solving skills
- Ensure all team members grow technically and professionally throughout the project



## 2. Common Values and Standards
We commit to the following core values:

- **Clear Communication** – Share updates, blockers, and progress openly and promptly  
- **Accountability** – Take ownership of assigned tasks and responsibilities  
- **Respect & Support** – Foster a positive, inclusive, and supportive team environment  
- **Reliability** – Meet deadlines and follow through on commitments  
- **Growth Mindset** – Be open to learning, feedback, and improvement  
- **Quality Work** – Strive for clean, efficient, and well-tested code  



## 3. Roles, Responsibilities, and Accountability
Roles will be **flexible and may rotate** depending on the task and project phase.

### General Roles
- **Team Lead**  
  - Organizes meetings and communicated with course staff
  - Posts weekly summaries and task assignments (rotates between leads)

- **Developers**  
  - Implement features and complete assigned tasks in accordance to the timeline

- **Note-Taker (rotates)**  
  - Records meeting notes, documenting the conversation, and later uploads it to GitHub

### Accountability Strcuture

To Ensure Fair and Equal Contributions & Optimal Team Growth:

- Track contributions through task assignments and commits  
- Maintain meeting notes documenting participation  
- Adjust workload distribution if imbalance occurs  
- Pair less experienced members with more experienced members when needed  

## 4. Potential Issues & Conflict Resolution

### Potential Issues
- Missed deadlines or incomplete work  
- Unequal contribution  
- Lack of communication  
- Skill gaps or uncertainty in tasks  

### Conflict Resolution Strategy
1. Address the issue early and respectfully within the team  
2. Discuss the problem openly and propose solutions  
3. Reassign or redistribute tasks if necessary  
4. If unresolved, reach out to the TA or instructor



## 5. Handling Violations of Team Rules

### Escalation Process
1. **First Instance** – Informal discussion and reminder  
2. **Second Instance** – Formal team discussion and documented warning  
3. **Third Instance** – Escalation to TA/instructor  


## 6. Communication Protocols

- Primary communication platform: **Slack**  
- Important updates, deadlines, and summaries will be posted in Slack  
- Expected response time: **within 24 hours**

### Meetings
- Weekly full-team meetings (in person starting Week 4)  
- Zoom allowed for extenuating circumstances  
- Additional sub-meetings scheduled as needed (meeting format at the discretion of the sub-team) 

### Attendance Policy
- Weekly meetings are **mandatory (1 hour)**  
- Notify the team **at least 24 hours in advance** if absent  
- More than **3 unexcused absences** will result in the execution of the escalation process

### Post-Meeting Updates (Responsibility of the Team Leads)
Each weekly summary will include:
- Key decisions  
- Assigned tasks  
- Deadlines  


## 7. Needs and Expectations

To succeed, we expect:
- Consistent participation and effort from all members  
- Honest communication about workload and availability  
- Willingness to help others and ask for help when needed  

We will:
- Respect each other’s schedules  
- Communicate availability and conflicts early  
- Support teammates in areas where they need improvement  


## 8. Working Methods and Processes

- Break projects into **clear subtasks**  
- Assign tasks based on **skills and learning goals**  
- Use **Git** for version control and collaboration  
- Conduct **code reviews** before final integration  
- Maintain organized documentation  


## 9. Measuring Success

We will measure success by:
- Meeting deadlines consistently  
- Delivering features that meet the requirements  
- Maintaining clean, functional, and tested code  
- Demonstrating strong teamwork and communication


## 10. Continuous Improvement

We will periodically review this contract and update it as needed to improve team effectiveness.
